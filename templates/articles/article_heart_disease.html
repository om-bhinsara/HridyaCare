Understanding Heart Disease

A comprehensive guide to prevention, symptoms, and management

Heart disease describes a range of conditions that affect your heart. This includes blood vessel diseases, heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias), and congenital heart defects.

Key Risk Factors

High blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, and physical inactivity are major contributors. Early management significantly reduces cardiovascular risk.

Prevention & Management

While genetic factors cannot be changed, you can prevent or manage most forms of heart disease through lifestyle choices:

  • Heart-healthy diet: Low sodium, high fiber, and rich in fruits & vegetables
  • Regular exercise: Aim for 150 minutes of moderate activity weekly
  • Stress management: Use tools like our Stress Assessment
  • Regular monitoring: Check blood pressure and cholesterol regularly
  • Smoking cessation: The single most preventable cause

Proactive Approach: Regular health check-ups combined with lifestyle modifications can reduce heart disease risk by up to 90%.
