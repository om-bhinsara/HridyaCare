Heart Health Dictionary

Resting Heart Rate (RHR)

Your resting heart rate is the number of times your heart beats per minute while you are at complete rest. It is a critical indicator of your overall heart health and cardiovascular fitness.

Key Takeaway: A lower resting heart rate generally implies better heart function and cardiovascular fitness.

What is "Normal"?

While the standard "normal" range is widely cited as 60–100 BPM, optimal health is often found at the lower end of that spectrum.

Category Beats Per Minute (BPM)
Athletes 40 – 60 BPM
Excellent 60 – 69 BPM
Average 70 – 79 BPM
Below Average 80+ BPM

Factors That Affect RHR

Your heart rate fluctuates based on several internal and external factors:

  • Stress & Anxiety: Adrenaline releases can temporarily spike your rate.
  • Medication: Beta-blockers may lower it, while thyroid meds may raise it.
  • Hydration: Dehydration forces the heart to work harder to pump blood.
  • Temperature: High humidity or heat can increase RHR by 5–10 BPM.

How to Measure Accurately

To get your true resting heart rate, do not measure it after coffee or exercise. The best time is right after you wake up, before getting out of bed.

⚠️ When to see a doctor
Consult a professional if your resting rate is consistently above 100 BPM (Tachycardia) or below 50 BPM (Bradycardia) if you are not a trained athlete, especially if accompanied by dizziness or fainting.
