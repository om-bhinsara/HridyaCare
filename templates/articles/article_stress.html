The Stress-Heart Connection

Chronic stress is often called the "silent killer" because of its stealthy impact on cardiovascular health. When you are stressed, your body releases cortisol and adrenaline.

Immediate Effects: Your blood vessels constrict and your heart rate rises. This is the natural "fight or flight" response.

Long Term Impact: If this response happens constantly (chronic stress), it can lead to hypertension, arterial damage, and irregular heart rhythms.

"Managing your mind is just as important as exercising your body for heart health."
