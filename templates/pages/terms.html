1. Nature & Purpose of HridyaCare

HridyaCare is designed to support wellness awareness, self-monitoring, and health education. The platform utilizes the device's camera and flash to detect blood volume changes in the fingertip to estimate physiological trends such as heart rate.

The system is intended for informational and educational use and should not be interpreted as a clinical, diagnostic, or therapeutic tool.

2. Scientific Basis & Accuracy

HridyaCare utilizes Photoplethysmography (PPG), a non-invasive optical technique that detects volumetric changes in blood circulation at the surface of the skin.

Smartphone camera-based PPG estimates typically have a margin of error of ±5 to ±10 BPM compared to clinical-grade pulse oximeters.

Accuracy depends on proper usage and external variables:

  • Finger Placement: The index finger must fully cover the camera lens.
  • Flashlight Usage: The torch/flash is required to illuminate the capillary bed.
  • Pressure: Pressing too hard restricts blood flow, while pressing too lightly lets in external light.
  • Body Temperature: Cold fingers (vasoconstriction) may result in weak signals.
  • Stability: Excessive hand movement during the scan will distort the signal.
  • Environmental Light: Strong ambient light can interfere with the PPG signal.

4. User Responsibilities

To ensure the most accurate results and safe usage:

  • Gently place the tip of your index finger over the rear camera lens.
  • Ensure the flashlight is ON during measurement (the app controls this automatically).
  • Do not press hard; a light touch is sufficient to detect a pulse.
  • Remain still and quiet for the duration of the scan (approx. 30 seconds).
  • Warm your hands before measurement if they are cold.
  • Use the data for personal wellness curiosity only.
  • Do not use while driving or operating machinery.
  • Consult healthcare professionals for medical concerns.
